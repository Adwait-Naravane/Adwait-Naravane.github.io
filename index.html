About

I’m a doctoral researcher in the QuantumGroup@UGent, funded by an FWO Doctoral Fellowship. My day-to-day is a mix of deriving the right tensor-network formulation for a physics problem and then writing the Julia that actually makes it run at scale.

Before Ghent I did my BS-MS at IISER Kolkata, with research stints that shaped how I work: a master’s thesis at TIFR on PEPS and DMRG for 2D lattices, a summer at RIKEN CCS running MPS methods for fermionic systems on the Fugaku supercomputer, and earlier work at TIFR combining Monte Carlo with neural networks to detect phase transitions.

I also teach — I supervise a master’s thesis and TA advanced physics courses — and I care about research being reproducible, which is why most of my output is open-source. Outside the office I cycle, go to the gym, and I’m always up for a good board game night.
